I am a student researcher committed to environmental science with a focus on community-based conservation and water protection. I study at Notre Dame College in Dhaka, and my work brings together scientific methods, traditional ecological knowledge, and the real voices of communities who live closest to environmental change.

My interest began in my hometown of Katashur, where I watched the Buriganga River shift from a healthy waterway into a polluted and harmful ecosystem. Seeing this transformation, and hearing a young boy ask why the same water that once supported his village now made people sick, shaped my purpose. It raised a question that guides my work: How can local communities lead restoration efforts through both their knowledge and scientific tools?

To explore this question, I created the Katashur Water Protection Initiative. Working with families, elders, and students, our team tested water, mapped pollution sources, and designed simple community-led solutions. Over 18 months, water quality improved by about 30 percent, and eight surrounding communities adopted our model for their own river restoration efforts.

A central part of my approach is ensuring that research is carried out with communities rather than on them. Our Water Guardians program blends traditional ways of reading the river with scientific monitoring tools. Through shared learning and local leadership, we show how meaningful and lasting environmental change grows from within the community itself.

Research Projects

Katashur Water Protection Initiative: A Participatory Framework for Riverine Restoration

Student Researcher & Notre Dame Ecospace Club Ambassador | 2022-Present
Key Achievements:
30% Water Quality Improvement 8 Communities Adopted Model 400% Increased Participation Harvard Partnership

Story: This project is one of the most meaningful experiences of my life. I first started working with professors at Notre Dame College and members of our environmental club, where I met researchers studying pollution in the Buriganga River. Listening to their stories and seeing their data opened my eyes to how deeply the river shaped people's lives. Later, our team received an email from a Harvard professor who wanted help designing a learning program for villagers living along the Buriganga. That moment made me realize that environmental science can connect communities across the world. Since then, I have continued this work and now conduct research under Professor James S. Kim, focusing on how local people and scientific tools can work together to protect the environment.

Research Question: Can community-led conservation models improve water quality in measurable ways while also preserving the traditional ecological knowledge that communities already hold?

Methodology: Mixed-methods participatory action research that brings together two approaches: collecting scientific water-quality data and learning directly from the stories, observations, and daily experiences of community members.

Status: Manuscript in preparation for Environmental Science and Policy.

EcoLens: Inclusive Climate Data Visualization for Visually Impaired Students

Laboratory Assistant | 2023-Present
Innovation Highlights:
Audio Sonification Haptic Feedback Screen Reader Optimized Co-Creation Design

Story: EcoLens began when I was tutoring visually impaired students in basic climate science. Many of them told me that climate graphs, maps, and charts felt "invisible" to them. That moment pushed me to create a tool that did not rely only on vision. Working with these students, I started designing a system where climate data could be heard, felt, and explored through accessible technology. This project made me realize that accessibility is not a side feature; it is something you build with the learners themselves.

Research Question: How can participatory design principles create accessible climate science tools that serve diverse learning communities?

Innovation: First climate data accessibility tool designed through co-creation with the students who would use it, blending audio cues, haptic feedback, and screen reader optimization.

Status: In beta testing; preparing demonstration for ACM ASSETS Conference.

Environmental Degradation & Public Health Outcomes in Rural Bangladesh

Student Research Assistant | Medical Mission Collaboration | 2023

Research Question: What are the direct pathways through which riverine pollution manifests as community health crises?

Fieldwork: 5 rural health camps organized across affected regions; collaboration with Dhaka Medical College Hospital. Conducted patient interviews (n=300+) while monitoring local water and air quality indicators.

Preliminary Findings: Strong correlation between proximity to contaminated water sources and increased incidence of waterborne illnesses, skin conditions, and respiratory complications.
